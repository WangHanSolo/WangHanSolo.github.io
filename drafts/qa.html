Q&A

Here are some common questions I get from youth groups.

  1. Youth: Why do you read the bible?
    Me: Life is like building a skyscaper, its important to have a blueprint.

  2. Students: What is the best way to study?
    Me: Study with joy.

  3. Youth: What side should I take on this controversial topic?
    Me: Take the side of listening and understanding.

  4. Youth: Is it easy to bully good people?
    Me: A good sheep always have a shepherd watching over.

  5. Youth: How do we live a good life among so much evil?
    Me: We can stay healthy even surrounded by germs

  6. Students: Why spend so much time to research a topic? Spending so much time on writing a thesis. Spending so much energy. What is the purpose?
    Me: If it is done with purpose, then the biggest goal is to understand what purpose is.


  7. Youth: Why does Jesus ride a lame donkey into Jeruselum and not a ferocious T-Rex?
    Me: Victory in humility, not pride.


  8. Youth: I keep thinking about my ex-girlfriend.
    Me: Your value doesn't decrease based on someone's inability to see your worth. What can you do now to start this new chapter with a bang?

  9. Youth: How do I walk out of disappointment?
    Me: Hope is like a piece of paper. It will be continuously torn apart. Yet, God is able to use the torn pieces to compose a more beautiful mosiac.


  10. Students: What is your most regretful moment?
    Me: Lift up the past to the Lord. He can turn past mistakes to beauty and glory.
